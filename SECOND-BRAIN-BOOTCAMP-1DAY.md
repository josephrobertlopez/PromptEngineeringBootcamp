# Second Brain Bootcamp
## One-Day Workshop | Internal

---

### Objective
**Walk out with a working AI context document built from your own extracted knowledge.**

### The Problem
- You have domain knowledge trapped in your head
- You write vague tickets, prompts, and specs
- You prompt AI from scratch every time
- AI gives generic answers because it lacks your context

### The Solution
**Morning:** Get it out of your head → linked notes
**Afternoon:** Package it for AI → reusable context doc

---

## Morning: Extraction (3 hours)

### What You'll Do
Turn tacit knowledge into structured, linked notes.

### The Flow

```
Step 1: Pick Your Domain (10 min)
└── What project/area do you work on daily?

Step 2: Brain Dump (30 min)
└── Facilitator-guided prompts
└── Write notes as you go (1 idea = 1 note)

Step 3: Add Links (20 min)
└── For each note: "What relates to this?"
└── Add 1-2 links per note

Step 4: Exercise — The Bad Ticket (30 min)
└── Write a Jira ticket cold (5 min)
└── Write the same ticket using your notes (10 min)
└── Compare with a partner (15 min)

Step 5: Fill Gaps (30 min)
└── What's missing? Add more notes.

Step 6: Graph Walk (20 min)
└── Pick a note, follow links 3 hops
└── What context emerged you didn't expect?
```

### You Leave With
- 15-25 linked notes covering your domain
- Experience: notes make artifacts clearer
- Preview: links surface hidden context

---

## Afternoon: Composition (3 hours)

### What You'll Do
Turn your notes into an AI context document you reuse everywhere.

### The Flow

```
Step 1: Audit Your Notes (20 min)
└── Which are "always relevant"?
└── Which are "sometimes relevant"?
└── Star the essentials.

Step 2: Compose Your Context Doc (45 min)
└── Use the template below
└── Pull from your starred notes
└── Follow links to find what you forgot

Step 3: Test It — Round 1 (30 min)
└── Pick a real task you need to do
└── Prompt AI with your context doc
└── Rate: How much correction needed?

Step 4: Iterate (30 min)
└── What was missing? Add it.
└── What was noise? Cut it.
└── Update your doc.

Step 5: Test It — Round 2 (30 min)
└── Different task, same context doc
└── Rate again. Should be better.

Step 6: The Aha Moment (25 min)
└── Same task, NO context doc
└── Same task, WITH context doc
└── Compare outputs. Feel the difference.
```

### You Leave With
- A working context document
- Tested on real tasks
- Ready to use tomorrow

---

## The Template

```markdown
# My Context

## Who I Am
[Your role, what you're responsible for, your expertise]

## My Domain
[What you work on, key constraints, main stakeholders]

## What Good Looks Like
[Your quality bar, format preferences, examples]

## Common Tasks
[What you do repeatedly, expected inputs/outputs]

## For AI
[What to assume, what to avoid, how to respond]
```

Keep it to 1-2 pages. Shorter = actually gets used.

---

## Why Links Matter

| Without Links | With Links |
|---------------|------------|
| You search "requirements" | You search "requirements" + follow connections |
| Get exactly what you asked for | Get what you asked for + related context |
| Miss things you forgot to search | Surface things you didn't know to ask |

**The point:** When this feeds RAG, links = graph traversal = better retrieval.
