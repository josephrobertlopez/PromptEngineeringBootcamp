# Second Brain Primer
## Pre-Read for Bootcamp Participants

---

## What's a Second Brain?

A system for storing knowledge **outside your head** so you can:
- Find it when you need it
- Connect ideas you'd otherwise forget
- Feed it to AI for better outputs

---

## Three Approaches

### 1. Zettelkasten (Luhmann)
**The academic's method**

| Note Type | Purpose |
|-----------|---------|
| Fleeting | Quick captures, temporary |
| Literature | Summaries in your own words |
| Permanent | Refined ideas, archived |

**The magic:** Links between notes. Forces you to see connections.

*70 books, 400 articles — one slip-box system.*

---

### 2. PARA (Forte)
**The productivity system**

| Folder | What Goes There |
|--------|-----------------|
| **P**rojects | Has a deadline |
| **A**reas | Ongoing responsibilities |
| **R**esources | Reference material |
| **A**rchive | Done / inactive |

**The magic:** Organized by actionability, not topic.

---

### 3. Da Vinci Style
**The creative's chaos**

- Messy journals
- Mixed observations + experiments + ideas
- No rigid structure
- Serendipitous connections

**The magic:** Low friction = more capture.

---

## The Hybrid (What Actually Works)

Most people combine:

| Analog | Digital |
|--------|---------|
| Paper for thinking | App for search |
| Sketches, mind maps | Tags, links |
| Morning pages | Structured notes |
| Quick captures | Long-form |

**The bridge:** Photo your paper notes → searchable in digital.

---

## Core Principles

### 1. Capture Everything
Don't filter at capture time. Write it down. Decide later.

### 2. One Idea = One Note
Atomic notes. Easy to link, easy to find.

### 3. Link Liberally
Ask: "What does this relate to?" Add the link.

### 4. Review Regularly
Notes you never revisit = notes that don't exist.

### 5. Start Small
10 minutes a day. Notice things. Write them down.

## Why This Matters for AI

| Without Second Brain | With Second Brain |
|---------------------|-------------------|
| Prompt from scratch | Prompt from notes |
| Generic AI outputs | Contextual AI outputs |
| Knowledge trapped in head | Knowledge retrievable |
| Flat search | Graph traversal |

**The link structure IS the RAG advantage.**

## Before the Bootcamp

Try this for one week:

1. **Capture 3 things daily** — observations, learnings, ideas
2. **One idea per note** — keep them atomic
3. **Add one link per note** — "what relates to this?"
4. **Use any tool** — paper, Apple Notes, Obsidian, whatever

Come to the bootcamp with 15-20 notes. We'll structure them.
